## 🚀 Getting Started

Once you open the software, follow these steps to configure your monitoring:

1. **Add a Device:** Click the plus icon in the top corner of the main window.
2. **Enter Details:** Type the name of your device, like "Office Printer."
3. **Input Address:** Enter the IP address of the device. This is a sequence of numbers (e.g., 192.168.1.1) that identifies the hardware on your network.
4. **Save:** Click the save button to add the device to your list.
5. **Monitor:** You will see a green light next to the device name if it is online. If the light turns red, the device is unreachable.

## 📉 Regular Maintenance

InfraMon runs automatically when you start your computer. You can minimize the window to your system tray. This keeps the icon in the bottom right corner of your screen near the clock. 

If you need to change your settings, right-click the InfraMon icon in the system tray and select "Open." You may view logs by selecting "History" from the application menu. These logs show when a device lost connection and when it came back online. 

## 🛡️ Security and Privacy

This application processes data locally on your machine. Your network information does not leave your computer. We do not track your activity or collect personal data. The software only sends "ping" requests to verify that your devices respond. These requests stay within your local network.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does the light turn red?**
A red light means the application cannot send a signal to the device. Check if the device is plugged into power. Ensure the device is connected to your router.

**Can I monitor devices on another network?**
InfraMon tracks devices on your current local network. It does not support remote monitoring across the internet by default.

**Is there a cost to use this software?**
No. This tool is free to use for personal and professional tasks. 

**Will this software slow down my computer?**
No. InfraMon uses very little memory. It operates quietly in the background without affecting your other desktop tasks.

**How do I uninstall the software?**
Open your Windows Settings, go to Apps, find InfraMon in the list, and select Uninstall. This removes all program files and settings from your computer.

## 💡 Best Practices for Network Monitoring

Keep your device list organized by giving each entry a descriptive name. If you use a laptop, note that the light will turn red when you disconnect from the network. This is expected behavior and does not indicate a device failure. Check your router status if many devices show a red light at the same time. Frequent red pulses from a single device may indicate a loose cable or failing hardware. Use the export feature in the settings menu to save a backup of your device list if you plan to move to a new computer.
